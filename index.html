Bio

I lead product work in responsible AI and privacy-first personalization.

I am a product leader at Google working on responsible AI and privacy-first personalization. My work sits at the intersection of product strategy, governance, user trust, and systems that handle personal context with care.

I have spent more than two decades building consumer and platform products across communications, mobility, e-commerce, AdTech, and privacy-sensitive technology. I turn ambiguous spaces into clear product architecture and help teams make durable decisions under real constraints.

Outside my Google work, I am building Lifaria, a private Life Book beta, serving on the TheatreWorks Silicon Valley Board of Trustees, and keeping a weekly bilingual haiku practice in English and Russian.

I work in Product Leadership at Google, with a focus on responsible AI and privacy-first personalization. My work asks a practical question: when products remember, infer, recommend, or adapt, how do we make those behaviors useful, governed, and legible to the person using them?

Over more than two decades, I have led product work across consumer technology, communications, mobility, e-commerce, AdTech, and platform systems. I have worked in startups, growth-stage companies, and enterprise environments, which has taught me how to move from ambiguity to product architecture, from strong opinion to testable strategy, and from isolated feature work to systems that can be operated responsibly.

Much of my current attention is on the boundaries around AI: what context a system should use, what it should forget, when it should ask permission, how it should explain itself, and where human judgment must stay in the loop. I care about building products that can earn trust over time, not just demonstrate intelligence in a moment.

Lifaria is my personal beta project around those ideas. It turns selected photos, notes, documents, and voice into a private Life Book, with sources, edits, and approval visible at each step. It is a product experiment, and also a way of thinking about memory, authorship, and the right role of AI in intimate material.

Outside product work, I serve on the TheatreWorks Silicon Valley Board of Trustees and write weekly bilingual haiku in English and Russian. Theatre keeps me close to timing, attention, and human presence; poetry keeps me honest about compression, image, and meaning.

Lifaria beta

A private Life Book beta built around sources and consent.

I am building Lifaria to turn selected photos, notes, files, and voice into meaningful moments, eras, and chapters. I want AI to serve the person whose life is being shaped, not claim the story for itself.

Early product shape

I am focusing the beta on a few hard things: keeping sources visible, letting people accept or reject AI suggestions, and making draft chapters editable.

Source-backed

I keep sources attached

I keep moments and chapters connected to the photos, files, notes, or memories that support them.

Human approval

I keep approval with the person

I am designing Lifaria around review, acceptance, editing, and ownership rather than automatic biography.

Private by design

Memory needs restraint

I am taking the product toward consent, inspection, and personal control over extraction or spectacle.

Life structure

I turn fragments into chapters

I shape sources into moments, moments into suggested eras, and eras into a Life Book with a coherent spine.

TheatreWorks / practice

What theatre keeps teaching me about attention.

I serve on the TheatreWorks Silicon Valley Board of Trustees, supporting the organization’s mission, long-term sustainability, and community role. The work brings my product and technology judgment into a cultural setting where the measure is not only efficiency, but resonance.

Theatre sharpens the same instincts required in product leadership: pacing, stakes, audience trust, emotional truth, and the courage to revise until the room changes.
